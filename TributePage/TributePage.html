Lalitaditya Muktapida

Lalitaditya Muktapida, the famous king of Kashmir from the Karkota dynasty. He was a great conqueror and a patron of culture and religion.

Central temple of the Martand Sun Temple built by Lalitaditya Muktapida.

Here is a more detailed timeline of his life and achievements:

  • 724 CE - Lalitaditya becomes the king of Kashmir after his father Durlabhaka (Pratapaditya II) dies. He inherits a strong and prosperous kingdom, and begins his military campaigns to conquer new territories
  • 725-730 CE - Lalitaditya defeats Yashovarman, the king of Kannauj, and annexes his kingdom. He also subjugates several other rulers in central and eastern India, such as the kings of Magadha, Kamarupa, Gauda, Kalinga and Andhra. He establishes his supremacy over most of northern India
  • 731-735 CE - Lalitaditya allies with the Tang dynasty of China against the Tibetan empire, which is threatening both their borders. He sends troops and supplies to assist the Chinese army in fighting the Tibetans. He also attacks and defeats the Turks in the southern Hindukush region
  • 736-740 CE - Lalitaditya turns his attention to the west and northwest, where he faces the Arab invaders who are trying to spread Islam in India. He defeats them in several battles, and prevents them from advancing further into India. He also conquers parts of present-day Afghanistan and Central Asia, such as Kabul, Zabulistan, Sindh, Baluchistan, Khorasan and Transoxiana. He becomes a world-renowned conqueror and a protector of Hinduism
  • 741-760 CE - Lalitaditya consolidates his empire and focuses on administration, culture and religion. He builds many temples, shrines, towns and forts in Kashmir and other parts of his realm. He patronizes art, literature, science and education. He builds the famous Martand Sun Temple, which is dedicated to Surya, the sun god. He also establishes a new capital at Parihasapura, near Srinagar. He dies in 760 CE, leaving behind a vast and glorious empire

Lalitaditya Muktapida was a remarkable ruler who left a lasting impression on the history of Kashmir and India. He is praised by Kalhana, the 12th century chronicler of Kashmir, as a “world conqueror” and a “miraculous king” in his Rajatarangini. He is also remembered as a hero in Rajasthan. He was undoubtedly one of the most powerful and influential kings of ancient India.
